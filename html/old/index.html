SLS - The most powerful rocket... EVER.

NASA’s Space Launch System will be the most powerful rocket we’ve ever built. When completed, SLS will enable astronauts to begin their journey to explore destinations far into the solar system. Production of the five-segment powerhouse motors for the Space Launch System (SLS) solid rocket boosters is on target at prime contractor Orbital ATK’s facilities in Utah, with 10 motor segments cast with propellant and four of those segments complete. Following propellant casting, the finished segments were evaluated using non-destructive techniques, such as x-ray, to ensure they met quality standards, and the exterior cases were painted white with black-and-white photogrammetric markings. All motor segments will ultimately be shipped to Kennedy Space Center, where they will be integrated with forward and aft booster structures and then with the SLS core stage.

Niel deGrasse Tyson

Niel deGrasse Tyson (born October 5, 1958) is an American astrophysicist, author, actor, and science communicator. He is the Director of the Hayden Planetarium in New York City, New York. Tyson founded the Department of Astrophysics in 1997 there, and has been a reasearch associate since 2003. Born and rasied in New York City, Tyson become fond of astronomy at the age of nine after a visit to the very same planetarium he is now the director of. He graduated from Bronx High School of Science and completed is bachelor's degree in physics at Harvard University. He went on to receive his master's degree in astronomy at the University of Texas at Austin. He also earned his doctorate and another master's in astrophysics at Columbia Univserity.

Tyson is an advocate for expanding the operations of the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA). Arguing that "the most powerful agency on the dreams of a nation is currently underfunded to do what it needs to be doing". Tyson also hosts a television show called "Star Talk" in which he and his guests discuss prominent scientific thought and theory.
